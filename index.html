Research Interests: In graduate school, I studied the theoretical foundations of heat flow and electric current on rough, intricate shapes called fractals. This uses a lot of different tools - probability theory, calculus, linear algebra, geometry, and more. Most of my research is theoretical but I do do some numerical / computational work. My PhD advisor was Luke Rogers. I am also expanding my research and knowledge areas. I am currently working on my data science, actuarial science, abstract algebra, and statistics skills. I also do some work related to deaf mathematics education and signing mathematics. Outside of math I like linguistics, economics, and history.
